Journey / BRICS 2026

India Is Calling Me

March 2026 · New Delhi, India

Today, I came back from one of the most special trips I have ever had so far.

Going to India, I attended the BRICS CCI Women Empowerment Annual Summit & Felicitations 2026, which I believe will be a turning point in my life. Meeting so many women from all around the world, all doing their best to lift other women, was truly impressive.

Let's remember that about a century ago, women had no right to vote, and now they are not only part of the future — they are shaping it. They are decision makers and strong leaders of countries.

At the BRICS CCI Annual Summit & Felicitations 2026

I was honored to be nominated as the 2nd runner-up of the Global Women Leadership Program (GWLP), which made this experience even more meaningful for me.

Certificate and recognition award for the Global Women Leadership Programme

Although I have been privileged to be recognized, there are thousands of talented girls and women who remain hidden, and may remain hidden forever. The world will become more equitable if those who are able actively identify such talents and connect them with opportunities for success.

Even one woman rising is a revolution. — Ms. Meenakshi

With incredible people I met at the summit

I am very thankful to the GWLP team, specially Ms. Ruby Sinha for her professional mentorship, and Ms. Leena Abu-Mukh Zoabi. I also extend my thanks to Mr. Sharad Malhotra for his revolutionary vision of empowering women and young entrepreneurs. Women need more and more supportive men around them to keep the pace and grow hand in hand.

New friendships from around the world

At the end, I wish I could call my family in Iran and share my joy with them, but things are not going well in my country. Although I am pushing myself to stay strong, we are all human beings — we need peace, freedom, and a proper atmosphere to grow and follow our dreams.

I express my thoughts to all my Iranian fellows — those inside Iran under bombs and full digital blackout, and those outside trying to remain strong and fulfill their duties with tears in their eyes.

Still rising.

PS: India is beautiful.
